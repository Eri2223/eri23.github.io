What is Bullying?

Bullying Description:

Anyone can be the victim of bullying, and it can occur anywhere, including at school, online, and at work. Bullying can cause your child to sustain short-term behavioral health problems in addition to physical wounds that will eventually heal. A child's chance of getting depression is higher than it is for a child who does not endure verbal or physical bullying. In fact, according to one study, the effects of bullying throughout childhood, such as sadness, can remain for up to 40 years. Bullying-induced depression can create a wide variety of symptoms, and in severe cases, it can end in suicidal thoughts.

Bullying can lead to depression, which can have a long-term effect on a child. It can be distressing to see your child suffer from depression and bullying, and many parents are unsure of how to assist. However, parents may help their kids deal with bullying and depression by taking appropriate measures.

Signs of Bullying:

1. Unexplainable injuries

The individual may be seen with sudden bruises, cuts, or wounds.

2. Lost or destroyed items

Items of the individual such as clothing, books, electronics, or jewelry cannot be seen.

3. Sudden Feeling of being unwell

Frequent headaches or stomach aches, feeling sick or faking illness are present within the individual's behaviour.

4. Changes in eating habits

The individual may be noticed suddenly skipping meals or binge eating. The individual may come home from school hungry because they did not eat lunch.

5. Difficulty sleeping

The individual is having a hard time to sleep due to possible frequent nightmares, overthinking or anxiety.

6. Declining performance

The individual shows loss of interest in working, or not wanting to go do some work.

7. Being alone most of the time

The individual is seen avoiding friends or social gatherings and situations.

8. Feelings of helplessness

The individual can be noticed overthinking and acting problematic most of the time.

9. Decreased self esteem

The individual becomes silent, and can be noticed to avoid any eye-to-eye contact.

What to do if you have Depression:

1. Take breaks

Consider limiting news to just a couple of times a day and disconnecting from phone, tv, and computer screens for a while.

2. Take care of yourself

Eat healthy, exercise, get plenty of sleep, and give yourself a break if you feel stressed out.

3. Take care of your body.

Take deep breaths, stretch, or meditate.

4. Make time to unwind

Try to do some other activities you enjoy.

5. Talk to others.

Talk with people you trust about your concerns and how you are feeling. Share your problems and how you are feeling and coping.

6. Avoid drugs and alcohol

These may seem to help, but they can create additional problems and increase the stress you are already feeling.

7. Recognize when you need more help

If problems continue or you are thinking about suicide, talk to a psychologist, social worker, or professional counselor.

8. Write it out

Write everything that has been bothering you to release some negativity.

9. Be more vocal

Speak out what you are feeling.
