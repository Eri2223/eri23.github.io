What is Depression?

Depression Description:

Depression is a widespread and dangerous medical condition that has an adverse impact on how you feel, think, and behave. Thankfully, it is also curable. Sadness and/or a loss of interest in previous hobbies are symptoms of depression. It can impair your ability to perform at work and at home and cause a number of mental and physical issues.

It has numerous triggers and a wide range of potential causes. An traumatic or stressful life event, such as a death in the family, a divorce, an illness,Â unemployment, or concerns about one's career or finances, may be the culprit for some people. Depression frequently results from a combination of many reasons.

Signs of Depression:

1. Feeling sad or having a depressed mood

2. Loss of interest or pleasure in activities once enjoyed

3. Changes in appetite

4. Sleeping Problems

5. Loss of energy

6. Increase in purposeless physical activity

7. Feeling worthless or guilty

8. Difficulty thinking, concentrating or making decisions

9. Thoughts of death or suicide

What to do if you have Depression:

1. Take breaks

Consider limiting news to just a couple of times a day and disconnecting from phone, tv, and computer screens for a while.

2. Take care of yourself

Eat healthy, exercise, get plenty of sleep, and give yourself a break if you feel stressed out.

3. Take care of your body.

Take deep breaths, stretch, or meditate.

4. Make time to unwind

Try to do some other activities you enjoy.

5. Talk to others.

Talk with people you trust about your concerns and how you are feeling. Share your problems and how you are feeling and coping.

6. Avoid drugs and alcohol

These may seem to help, but they can create additional problems and increase the stress you are already feeling.

7. Recognize when you need more help

If problems continue or you are thinking about suicide, talk to a psychologist, social worker, or professional counselor.

8. Write it out

Write everything that has been bothering you to release some negativity.

9. Be more vocal

Speak out what you are feeling.
