What is Kidnapping?

Kidnapping Description:

In criminal law, kidnapping is the unlawful confinement of a person against their will, often including transportation/asportation.

The asportation and abduction element is typically but not necessarily conducted by means of force or fear. The perpetrator may use a weapon to force the victim into a vehicle, but it is still kidnapping if the victim is enticed to enter the vehicle willingly.

Kidnapping may be done to demand for ransom in exchange for releasing the victim, or for other illegal purposes.

Kidnapping can be accompanied by bodily injury which elevates the crime to aggravated kidnapping. Kidnapping of a child is known as child abduction, which is a separate legal category.

Signs of Kidnapping:

1. A stranger suddenly asking for a helping hand

It may seem harmless, but this is one of the most used tactics in kidnapping. A person asks for help about something then proceeds to lead you to the trap.

2. Sudden offer of gifts

As great as it seems, gifts can be a way of gaslighting used by kidnappers.

3. Strangers offering a free ride

Free rides are highly dangerous, keep your guards up especially during nights.

4. A vehicle slowly following behind

If you notice a vehicle following you to every curve you take alert the police or someone you trust to help out.

5. A stranger tailing behind

If a person follows you everywhere you go, it's time to ask for help. Find trustworthy individuals such as the police and security guards.

6. Unconmfortable behavior of one person in a group

If you notice a person acting strange and uncomfy in a group of people alert the authorities. This behavior has a high chance that the person acting strange is kidnapped.

What to do if you are Kidnapped:

1. Run away from someone who tries to take you if you can

2. Yell to draw attention to the situation

3. Fight your attacker as hard as you can

4. Look for objects in your environment to use as weapons

5. Follow your captors' instructions once you are in captivity

6. Stay calm and focused on survival

7. Try to connect with your kidnappers, but don't pretend to support them

8. Observe as much of your environment as you can

9. Find a way to communicate if there are other captives
