What is Racism?

Racism Description:

Racism is the idea that certain human populations have distinct behavioral features related to genetic characteristics and can be classified according to the superiority of one race over another.

Prejudice, discrimination, or hostility aimed at a person or persons because they belong to a specific racial or ethnic group,

Types of Racism:

1. Casual or indirect Racism:

Microaggressions are another form of covert or "casual" racism. This inflammatory message, whether deliberate or not, targets a person just because they belong to a minority group. Racism in any form is unacceptable, especially when it is implied or takes place in a relaxed setting. Just not appropriate.

Examples of Casual Racism

consciously deciding not to sit next to someone because you find their skin color offensive

claiming that someone who was born and reared in Australia and is of a different race speaks "excellent English"

sneering at someone's history, even if it's done in jest.

2. Direct Racism:

This kind of racism is deliberate and premeditated; an example would be someone posting negatively about a certain ethnic community on Instagram.

3. Systemic Racism:

When institutions in our society, such as the government, media outlets, police, hospitals, and schools, discriminate against particular groups of people, it is considered this sort of racism. As it was stated before, this is a result of past institutions and power. For instance, the global Black Lives Matter movement was created as a direct reaction to police abuse of black people, including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islanders.
