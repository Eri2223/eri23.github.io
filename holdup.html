What is Holdup?

Holdup Description:

A holdup is a particular kind of robbery that is carried out with the use of a lethal or hazardous weapon. It is also referred to as a stick up occasionally.

The usual order given by a robber to a victim to stick or hold their hands up in the air so they cannot protect themselves gave rise to the expression "stick up." A knife or a rifle is frequently employed as a weapon.

Different places where Holdup occurs:

1. Robbery at Automated Teller Machines

The most serious issue with their presence is a robbery of a person using an ATM. A would-be thief will undoubtedly be aware that someone using an ATM will typically exit the device with cash in hand after withdrawing funds. In addition, various personal items like wallets, handbags, credit cards, and jewelry are frequently taken from victims of ATM robberies.

2. Robbery of Convenience Stores

The bigger problem of robbery and commercial businesses includes many issues, of which convenience store crime is only one aspect. Even if there are certain similarities across all robbery types, convenience store robberies need special consideration because of their special attributes.

3. Home Invasion Robbery

Home invasion robberies are merely one issue among several that are connected to violent crime in residential areas. This manual only addresses the specific harm brought on by home invasion robberies.

4. Robbery of Taxi Drivers

The absence of information especially gathered on cab driver robberies is one of the main obstacles to comprehending the issue. Most police forces don't keep records of robbery incidents that make it simple to distinguish between those involving taxis. The data constantly reveal that taxi drivers have the highest or among the highest risk of job-related homicide and assault of any occupation or industry. This information is the foundation for much of what is known about taxi robbery.

5. Bank Robbery

The problem of bank robberies is just one of several issues connected to robberies and other financial crimes involving banks.

6. Crimes Against Tourists

The most frequent crime committed against tourists is robbery, but they are also susceptible to scams, physical and sexual assault, credit card fraud, and other crimes.

7. Body language of a robber

Always be on the lookout for anything strange. Look for an item that doesn't belong. Not all thieves strike with confidence; some may have uneasy body language, such as shifty eyes, unfocused gaze, or continuously scanning the area to check if anyone is nearby.

8. Ease of entrance

The likelihood that a burglar will enter your home increases with the ease with which they can do so. That's not to say that locking your doors and windows is a sufficient deterrent to stop all burglaries, but it's a place to start.

9. You're on Crowded Public Transport

A pickpocket's paradise, public transportation. There are so many people bumping into you and hiding places for hands to slip into pockets. In a big city, this is prime territory for robberies.

What to do if you are being Holduped:

1. Remain calm and do not resist.

Assure the robber that you would cooperate and not do anything to endanger your safety. Avoid any erratic or fast movements.

2. Follow the robber’s directions, but do not offer more than what they ask for.

Avoid arguing. Inform the robber of what you are about to do and why, even if it means getting up or reaching into your pockets.

3. Make mental notes of the robber’s appearance.

When submitting your report, you must provide details about the suspect. Make a note of their physical characteristics, such as their race, age, height, and hair- and eye-color. Do they have any distinctive features, like scars or tattoos, on their body? If they are carrying a weapon, note it down so you can later describe it. Try to take down the brand, model, and license plate number of the vehicle if they arrive or depart in one.

4. Notice what the robber does so you can include it in your report.

Observe any communications that the two accomplices, if any, have with one another. Do they address one another by name or by a pet name? Do they refer to any specific places? Try to recall the items they touched during the heist so that the police can look for fingerprints there.

5. After the robbery, go immediately to a safe location, then report the crime.

Your main priority is to keep yourself safe. Before you dial 911 or DPS, make sure you feel safe. If you find yourself in a remote location, move to a populated place and ask a friend to stay with you while you wait for assistance. Try not to touch anything if you decide to stay at the crime site. Ask any witnesses to stick around until the police show up if there were any. Note their name, address, and phone number if they must leave.
