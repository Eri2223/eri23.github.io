What is Stress?

Stress Description:

Feeling agitated, overwhelmed, or simply tired to do anything even though the thing done for the day is sit and attentively listen to discussions? These are signs of stress. Every day, people encounter all sorts of demands. Variety of these demands are thrown to different individuals with desires popping from here to there. A person feels a certain kind of pressure, also known as stress.

Stress is how we react when we feel under pressure or threatened. It usually happens when we are in a situation that we don't feel we can manage or control.

At any time, anyone can experience stress. Life and stress are highly interconnected because life is composed of different events, each event having a possibility to trigger stress. Stress is simply a physical or mental response to something or someone. Stress may happen at any time and can either be short-term or long-term

Signs of Stress:

1. Worrying incessantly

Due to pressure, the individual tend to overthink the situation which sprouts negative mindset to oneself.

2. Feeling overwhelmed

The individual looses control of what is happening which makes the weight of situations heavy.

3. Dizziness

From constant worrying and thinking, the individual may feel dizzy from time to time.

4. Rapid heartbeat

Worriying and overthinking makes the individual feel fear which rapidly increases the heartbeat.

5. Moodiness

As the pressure felt by the individual continues, the mood is affected which tend to change rapidly.

6. Anxious

As stress lurks, anxiety develops which gives the feeling of unease and nervousness.

7. Using alcohol or drugs

Some individual turn to alcohol and drugs to somehow cope with the stress being felt.

8. Unable to concentrate

With constant overthinking fueled by pressure, the individual loose focus of what is happening.

9. Inability to relax

With pressure and the mind being bugged, the individual cannot have a clear mind.

What to do if you are Stressed:

1. Keep journal.

List what happened on a day-to-day basis to help keep track and reflect on your actions.

2. Write it out

Write what makes you stressed, what's on your mind. Let out your emotions to make the feeling lighter.

3. Drink plenty of water

Water has many benefits, drinking water lessens dehydration that may be pressent during stressful times.

4. Practice deep breathing

Deep breathing helps stimulate relaxation, casual practice may help in calming oneself.

5. Minimize phone use and screen time

Social media or any media out there may add up to the stress being felt. Take a break and do things that can help ease your mind

6. Give yourself a break

Sometimes rest can be the best remedy. Go sleep, and take time to do things that makes you happy.

7. Avoid drugs and alcohol

As much as it can help, drugs and alcohol will never be the best coping mechanism as it can develop new problems in the long run.

8. Meditate

Relax. It can make your mind clear up.

9. Unwind

Take time to take care of yourself.
